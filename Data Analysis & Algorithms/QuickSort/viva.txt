Quick Sort is a divide-and-conquer sorting algorithm that partitions the array around a pivot and recursively sorts the subarrays.

It selects a pivot element, rearranges elements smaller than the pivot to its left and larger to its right, then recursively sorts both halves.

Deterministic Quick Sort uses a fixed rule for pivot selection (like first or last element), which can lead to worst-case performance for certain inputs.

4Ô∏è‚É£ What is randomized Quick Sort?
It selects a random pivot each time, which reduces the probability of encountering the worst case, leading to average O(n log n) performance.

Quick Sort is in-place, requiring only O(log n) stack space on average for recursion.

A pivot divides the array into two parts. Choosing a good pivot ensures balanced partitions and faster sorting.

Common methods are Lomuto and Hoare partition schemes; both rearrange elements around the pivot.

Because it has excellent cache performance and low overhead compared to other O(n log n) sorts like Merge Sort.
